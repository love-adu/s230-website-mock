§ 01 — The Law

26 words that built — and broke — the internet

"No provider or user of an interactive computer service shall be treated as the publisher or speaker of any information provided by another information content provider."

— 47 U.S.C. § 230(c)(1), enacted 1996

Passed in 1996, Section 230 was designed to protect early internet companies from being crushed by lawsuits over what their users posted. Then, it worked — and the internet we know today was built on that foundation.

But the internet of 1996 and the internet of today are unrecognizable to each other. Back then, there were roughly 36 million internet users worldwide. Today there are over 5 billion. The platforms are no longer scrappy message boards — they are trillion-dollar corporations with algorithmic systems that actively amplify and recommend content.

The law has never been updated once. We pay the price.
